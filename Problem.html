The Problem

The world generates enough food to feed the entire world's population 1.5 times, which is enough to feed over 10 BILLION people, yet 8.9% of the world's population goes to bed every night with an empty stomach.



























Food Waste Statistics

One third of the food produced ends up in landfills. That's 1.3 billion metric tons every year.

The total cost of food waste is estimated to be $1 trillion every year (including seafood).

If food waste was a country, it would be the third-largest greenhouse gas producer; it is estimated that 3.3 billion tons of CO2 every year (about 1,000 tons of CO2 per minute).

Food Waste is Just the Tip of the Iceberg

About 690 million people around the world are starving, and they don't have access to proper food yet perfectly good food is wasted. This is simply unacceptable.

If even one-fourth of the food that was wasted is recovered, it would be enough to feed all the world's hungry.

Problems such as this won't go away by itself. People with leftovers, overcrowded fridges, and dinner plates filled to the brim need to be aware of what is going on.
